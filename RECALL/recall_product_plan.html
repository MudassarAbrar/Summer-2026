Recall
Save it once. Never forget it. Always act on it.
Mobile + Browser Extension AI-Powered Personal → Public Launch All Platforms
The Problem

You save dozens of videos, articles, posts, and links every week across TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, Instagram, and the web. They sit in watch-later lists, bookmarks, and starred folders for months — until the opportunity expires, the trend dies, or you simply forget. All that saving was wasted effort.

Core Loop
01
Capture from anywhere
Share any link via the mobile share sheet or browser extension. Works with TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, blogs, and any web URL. For long YouTube videos, you add a quick note — the AI uses the title + your note to categorize without needing to watch the whole thing.
02
AI reads and understands it
The app visits the link, fetches the content, and generates a structured summary card — what the content is about, key insights, and most importantly: any tools, apps, resources, or opportunities mentioned inside it. For YouTube, it uses Gemini's Ask Gemini feature to get a full video summary.
03
Auto-categorize with your own system
AI assigns a category automatically. You can override anytime or add custom categories. Every item lives in a searchable, filterable library — not just a flat list.
04
Smart reminders until you act
Recall keeps nudging you. Time-sensitive items (trending tools, limited opportunities, expiring offers) get flagged immediately. Everything else gets periodically surfaced — "You saved this AI tool 3 weeks ago and haven't used it yet." — until you mark it actioned.
05
Mark done — it never disappears
When you've acted on something, you mark it done. It moves to your archive — permanently. Your library is your memory, not a task list. You can always come back to a resource months later even after marking it done.

Enhanced Features (beyond the original idea)
Resource extraction
If a video or article mentions 5 tools, your summary card lists all 5. You see the tools before you even watch the video.
Freshness scoring
Each item gets a freshness tag. Tech news from 8 months ago gets flagged as likely outdated. Time-sensitive items get urgency labels automatically.
Cross-link detection
If you save 3 things about the same topic, Recall groups them. "You have 4 saved items about n8n automation — want to review them together?"
Weekly digest
Every Sunday, a digest of your unactioned items sorted by urgency, category, and time saved — so you can do a quick weekly review in one session.
Semantic search
Search by meaning, not just title. "Find that video about cold email" surfaces it even if you don't remember the exact words in the title.
Action notes
When you mark something done, you optionally write what you did. Builds a log of actions taken — useful for reflecting on what you actually executed on.
Action streak
Gamified motivation: tracks how many items you've actioned vs saved. Keeps you honest about your follow-through ratio over time.
Spaces (for public launch)
When you go public: shared workspaces. A team or community can share a Recall space — curate a group library of useful resources together.
Build Phases
Phase 1
Personal MVP. Browser extension + mobile share sheet. Link ingestion, AI summary + categorization, basic reminder system, archive on action. Just for you.
Phase 2
Smart layer. Resource extraction, freshness scoring, cross-linking, semantic search, weekly digest, Gemini integration for YouTube.
Phase 3
Public launch. Auth, user accounts, onboarding, Spaces for shared libraries, action streaks, public-facing polish.
Key Design Decisions

Never delete. Marking done moves to archive, never erases. Your library is permanent memory.

Reminders are persistent, not one-shot. Unlike every other app, Recall keeps nudging until you act. It's designed to fight the save-and-forget habit at its root.

Long videos use title + user note + Gemini. No need to process full video content — this keeps it fast and practical for YouTube.

Categories are AI-suggested, human-confirmed. You stay in control. AI is a starting point, not a dictator.
